\section*{\textbf{Problem}}
Question 2.17: \\ 
Three cards are drawn successively, without
replacement from a pack of 52 well shuffled
cards. What is the probability that first two
cards are kings and the third card drawn is an ace?
\section*{\textbf{Solution}}
Let $X_i$ where $i \in \{1,2,3\}$ be random variables which represent the outcome of the card drawn on the $ith$ turn such that the random varaibles $X_i$ take values $\{0,1,2\}$
\begin{enumerate}
    \item $X_i=0$ represents King card is drawn
    \item $X_i=1$ represents Ace card is drawn
    \item $X_i=2$ represents neither King nor Ace cards are drawn
\end{enumerate}
\bigskip
We need to find \pr{X_1=0,X_2=0,X_3=1)} \\
Using Conditional Probability and the fact that the Random Variables are independent of each other we can write
\begin{multline}
    \pr{X_1=0,X_2=0,X_3=1} = \pr{X_1=0}\times \\ \pr{X_2=0|X_1=0} \times \pr{X_3=1|X_1=0,X_2=0}
\end{multline}
\begin{align}
\begin{split}
 \pr{X_1=0,X_2=0,X_3=1} &=\textstyle\frac{4}{52} \times \textstyle\frac{3}{51} 
 \times \frac{4}{50} \\ &=0.000362
\end{split}
\end{align}

Therefore, the required Probability is 0.000362
